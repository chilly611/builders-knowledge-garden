# Competitive Brief: Killer App vs. Construction-Tech Landscape (Q1 2026)

**Date:** April 22, 2026  
**Product:** Builder's Knowledge Garden (BKG) — Killer App (Pro tier)  
**Primary User:** Small-to-mid-sized GC (1–20 crew), solo ops, $50k–$5M jobs  
**Status:** Positioning & messaging framework

---

## 1. Top-10 Competitor Profiles

### Procore
**Positioning:** Enterprise construction cloud for large contractors and full portfolios.  
**Target Segment:** Multi-project, multi-office enterprises; GCs with 50+ crew or $100M+ annual volume.  
**Pricing:** Volume-based annual fee (0.1%–0.2% of hard construction costs); typical range $10k–$50k+/year or $375–$4k+/month depending on size and modules selected. Unlimited users and storage included.  
**AI Posture:** Visualization and reporting automation, not AI-native. Procore emphasizes integrated workflows (preconstruction, project execution, quality/safety, financials) but does not lead with AI specialists or real-time decision support.  
**Where They Win:** Preconstruction estimating, document control, RFI/submittal management, team coordination at scale, integrations with ERP.  
**Where They Miss:** Overkill for crews under 20. No AI specialists that cite real local code or material data. Cost curve punishes small GCs; volume-based pricing scales upward fast.

### Buildertrend
**Positioning:** All-in-one home builder and remodeler platform (cloud CRM + project management + financials).  
**Target Segment:** Residential GCs and remodelers, 5–50 crew, custom and semi-custom homes.  
**Pricing:** Three tiers (Essential, Advanced, Complete); $399–$1,099/month ($399–$599 effective after annual discount). Unlimited users and projects.  
**AI Posture:** Semi-automated. AI-powered weekly client updates that pull from project data and generate summaries; AI photo-to-summary for client portals. Lacks predictive insights or specialist guidance; mostly data formatting.  
**Where They Win:** Client communication (portal, updates, eSignature), CRM, basic financials, team task assignment. Strong in custom remodeling workflows.  
**Where They Miss:** No local code or compliance guidance. AI is shallow—photo summaries and data sorting, not decision support. Struggles with mixed job types (remodel + service + maintenance).

### Knowify
**Positioning:** Construction accounting and project management for trade contractors and service work.  
**Target Segment:** Trade specialists (plumbing, HVAC, electrical, carpentry), 2–200 crew, recurring service + project work.  
**Pricing:** Core ($99–$149/month), Advanced, Enterprise; each additional user $10/month. Free trial available.  
**AI Posture:** Minimal. Form capture and photo organization; no AI specialists or code guidance.  
**Where They Win:** QuickBooks integration (two-way sync), AIA invoicing, time tracking, job costing, simple field photos and annotations. Strong for trade contractors doing service work.  
**Where They Miss:** No preconstruction support. No code compliance, no estimating, no AI decision support. Weak on large commercial jobs.

### JobTread
**Positioning:** Integrated estimating, job costing, and project management for small-to-mid GCs.  
**Target Segment:** 1–50 crew, mixed job types, $50k–$5M project range.  
**Pricing:** $199/month + $20/user. Subcontractors and customers free. Volume discounts after 10 users.  
**AI Posture:** None. Pure forms and workflows; no AI specialists, no predictive features.  
**Where They Win:** Estimating (cost catalog, quick markup), budgeting, job costing, change orders, strong for first-time software users. Flat-rate pricing (no per-user gouging). Mobile-friendly.  
**Where They Miss:** No code compliance, no AI guidance, no material sourcing support, no local regulation lookup. Estimating is rule-based, not AI-driven.

### Contractor Foreman
**Positioning:** Affordable, all-in-one job management for small GCs and crews.  
**Target Segment:** 1–20 crew, $50k–$2M jobs, cost-conscious.  
**Pricing:** $49–$249/month depending on tier; flat pricing per account (not per user). 30-day free trial.  
**AI Posture:** Minimal and emerging. AI chat support (human + bot hybrid), AI-powered form builder. Not AI-native; AI is a support and form-building accelerator, not decision guidance.  
**Where They Win:** Affordability, job costing, daily logs, scheduling, AIA invoicing, quick adoption (designed for crews that avoid software). Flat per-account pricing appeals to solopreneurs.  
**Where They Miss:** No preconstruction, no code compliance, no real AI specialists, no material sourcing. Form AI is generic; doesn't understand construction domain.

### CoConstruct
**Positioning:** Custom home builder software (now being sunset by Buildertrend).  
**Target Segment:** Custom residential GCs, high-end remodelers, 5–30 crew.  
**Pricing:** ~$399/month (still standalone as of 2026, but acquisition in 2021 has left it neglected).  
**AI Posture:** None. Legacy platform with minimal development since Buildertrend acquisition.  
**Where They Win:** Client portal, selections management, legacy customer base.  
**Where They Miss:** Buildertrend has explicitly stopped updating CoConstruct; support reduced to skeleton crew. Effectively deprecated. Users report 500% price hikes post-acquisition with no feature parity.

### Houzz Pro
**Positioning:** Design-first, all-in-one for architects, designers, and remodeling GCs.  
**Target Segment:** Design-forward remodelers, architects, interior designers; 1–50 crew.  
**Pricing:** Essential ($149/month), Pro, Custom; additional users $60/month. 30-day free trial.  
**AI Posture:** Minimal. 3D floor planner and client visualization tools; no AI for code compliance, estimating, or job execution.  
**Where They Win:** Design and visualization (3D floor plans, client selections), 3D rendering, portfolio management, designer-to-GC handoff. Strong for high-end residential remodeling.  
**Where They Miss:** No preconstruction estimating, no code compliance, no AI guidance, no field operations support. Weak for commercial or mid-size GCs; too design-centric.

### Fieldwire (by Hilti)
**Positioning:** Real-time field execution and progress tracking (acquired by Hilti in 2022).  
**Target Segment:** Jobsite crews, field supervision, progress documentation; 5–500 crew.  
**Pricing:** Free (5 users, 3 projects), Pro ($39–$49/user/month), Business and up.  
**AI Posture:** Emerging survey interest in AI on jobsite; no AI specialists or compliance guidance shipped yet. Focus is vision-based safety (AI analysis of video feeds for fall hazards, PPE).  
**Where They Win:** Field execution, real-time progress photos, punch lists, dailies, blueprints on mobile, safety documentation. Strong for large on-site teams and general contractors coordinating subs.  
**Where They Miss:** No preconstruction or code compliance. Jobsite-only; doesn't help with estimation, sourcing, or locked-scope planning. Requires larger crews to justify adoption.

### CompanyCam
**Positioning:** Photo + video documentation and AI-powered field reporting.  
**Target Segment:** Field teams, service contractors, GCs doing daily site documentation, 1–100+ crew.  
**Pricing:** Pro ($99/month), Premium ($149), Elite ($249), Enterprise (custom). Per-user pricing also available ($19–$29).  
**AI Posture:** AI-powered reporting (AI Reporting feature generates structured reports from job photos and voice notes). Photos are auto-organized and tagged. Dual video mode for close-up + wide shots. This is the most AI-forward of the field-focused tools, but AI is limited to report generation, not decision guidance.  
**Where They Win:** Photo and video capture with instant cloud sync, automated report generation from site photos, task and checklist assignment, geo-tagging and time-stamping.  
**Where They Miss:** No estimating, no code compliance, no preconstruction guidance, no job costing. AI is reporting-only; doesn't help plan or execute.

### Trimble Connect
**Positioning:** BIM-integrated collaboration and project coordination for large contractors and construction firms.  
**Target Segment:** Mid-to-enterprise contractors, 20–500+ crew, complex projects with BIM.  
**Pricing:** Free Personal plan, Pro and Enterprise; typical spend $500–$2k/user/year. Entry level $12.41. Enterprise discounts 10–25% for 50+ seats.  
**AI Posture:** None. Pure collaboration and BIM coordination; no AI specialists or decision support.  
**Where They Win:** BIM integration (Revit, digital twins), team coordination on large projects, document and model sharing, preconstruction visualization.  
**Where They Miss:** Not built for small GCs. High training overhead. No estimating, no code compliance, no field execution support, no job costing. AI-free zone.

## 3. Five Messaging Gaps BKG Can Credibly Claim

### 1. **AI That Cites Real Code**
**Claim:** We don't hallucinate code. Every compliance answer references ICC/IBC/IRC + NFPA + jurisdiction amendments with entity IDs and source stamps.  
**Why competitors can't copy it fast:** Requires structured database of 50+ jurisdictions' amendments, legal review, and continuous updates. Procore, Buildertrend, JobTread have no code data layer. CompanyCam's AI is photo-to-summary only. Building this is a 6–12 month library buildout; can't retrofit after launch.

### 2. **3-Source Verification (Seed + ICC + NFPA + Local)**
**Claim:** Every answer comes from three sources: BKG's proprietary data, standard bodies (ICC, NFPA), and local AHJ amendments. Cross-checked before we show it to you.  
**Why competitors can't copy it fast:** Requires partnerships (or licensing) with ICC/NFPA, legal liability insurance for code advice, and audit trails. Most competitors are purely generic; none have built multi-source verification into their stack. This is a moat, not a feature sprint.

### 3. **Voice-First Input (Not Voice-as-Afterthought)**
**Claim:** Job updates, daily logs, compliance checks—voice first, typing second. Built for crew members in hard hats, not office workers.  
**Why competitors can't copy it fast:** Requires domain-specific voice models (field noise, jargon, accent diversity), careful UX (voice transcription + confirmation loops), and testing on jobsite. Buildertrend and Fieldwire have mobile-first, but not voice-first. Adding voice later feels bolted-on because it is.

### 4. **Lifecycle Metaphor (Size Up → Lock → Plan → Build → Adapt → Collect → Reflect)**
**Claim:** We don't call it "project status." We call it a journey. Each stage has its own expert specialists, templates, and workflows. You navigate, not grind.  
**Why competitors can't copy it fast:** Requires rethinking the entire product mental model and UX hierarchy. Procore's preconstruction/execution/closeout is linear and forms-heavy. Fielwire's is execution-only. Buildertrend is CRM-to-project tunnel. Killer App's journey metaphor is not a UI element; it's the product architecture. Retrofitting takes a full rewrite.

### 5. **Database as the Moat (Not API Integrations)**
**Claim:** Every decision is grounded in our structured data (materials, suppliers, codes, crew benchmarks, past projects). We cite real entities, not hallucinated ones.  
**Why competitors can't copy it fast:** Most contech companies rely on API passthrough (Procore links to QuickBooks, Buildertrend links to Stripe). They don't own the decision layer. BKG's database is the product; decisions flow from data, not guesses. Building this requires domain expertise, partnerships, and ongoing curation. It's a strategic moat, not a feature.

## 5. Five "We Stand For / Against" One-Liners (Foreman Voice)

1. **"We don't track hours. We track jobs."**  
   Competitor obsession: timesheets, labor burden, crew utilization rates. Our obsession: Did we make money? Will the next job be better? What code did we miss?

2. **"Your AI specialist shouldn't hallucinate code. Ours cites it."**  
   We don't guess at IRC amendments. We show you the source. You quote it to your inspector. You sleep at night.

3. **"Voice first, because you're not at a desk."**  
   A crew member in a hard hat on a wet jobsite shouldn't tap out a daily log on a phone. They should talk into their phone and move on. We listen.

4. **"Compliance isn't legal-speak. It's a checklist in your workflow."**  
   Code compliance isn't a document to file away. It's built into how you plan the job, what you order, what you check before you build. We bake it in.

5. **"We didn't build a software company. We built a knowledge company that happens to be software."**  
   Database of jurisdictions, codes, materials, benchmarks, crew data. Software is how we share it. But the database is the real product. Everything else flows from real data.

## Summary: The Killer App Positioning

Killer App is **the AI-native operating system for small GCs who run jobs smart**. Not all-in-one like Procore, not pretty-UI like Houzz, not cheap-and-cheerful like Foreman. We're **deep in the craft**: code compliance that cites sources, material sourcing from real databases, crew benchmarking from BKG data, and all of it wired into a seven-stage lifecycle journey that matches how GCs actually think about projects.

The positioning is strong because it's defensible (the database, the multi-source model, the voice layer) and addresses a real gap: GCs under 20 crew are stuck between Contractor Foreman ($50–$150/month, no compliance, no estimating) and Procore ($10k–$50k/year, overkill, forms-first). Killer App lands at $200–$300/month with specialist guidance that neither can match in the next 12 months.

**Next priorities:**  
1. Lock 3-source code data layer and first two jurisdictions (pilot in high-volume states: CA, TX, FL).  
2. Ship first three workflows (Code Compliance Lookup, Contract Templates, Size Up) with real specialist wiring.  
3. Build battlecard sales deck and test positioning with 5–10 target GC pilots.  
4. Monitor Procore's AI roadmap and CompanyCam's expansion; adjust messaging if either ships specialist-level features.
