The Moat

The platform improves itself in public.

Every other platform in our space holds static data and ages. We get more right every week. That is the moat in the AI era.

The RSI Heartbeat is the platform. One self-improving knowledge graph per garden, ingesting source data on a domain cadence, re-verifying every entity, surfacing freshness on every claim, learning from use. The platform doesn't hold knowledge — it improves itself.

The Architecture

One picture. The whole platform.

Every garden inherits this skeleton. The skin changes per domain — codes versus species versus compounds versus case law — but the lanes, the surfaces, the heartbeat, and the federation contract do not.

Four umbrella lanes. Three surfaces. One self-improving graph beneath them all.

The Pattern

Four lanes ✕ three surfaces ✕ one graph.

Every garden in the platform composes from this skeleton. Sub-lanes nest inside the four umbrella lanes per domain — BKG has eight sub-lanes, HKG has five, OKG has six — but the four umbrella lanes are universal.

How to read this section

A piece is a reusable design pattern — like "always show a save button" but more specific. Build a workflow with the right pieces and it works for every kind of user, on every kind of device, in every kind of moment.

An axis is a way users differ from each other. There are fourteen. A homeowner and a master plumber differ on the skill axis. A worker logging from a muddy job site and a financier reviewing on a laptop differ on the device, modality, and tempo axes. The Stance Card is the live snapshot of where each user sits on all fourteen.

Each piece below shows: a plain-English explanation, three real examples from the Builder's Garden, which axes the piece touches (14 dots — lit = relevant; double-ring = primary), and why it matters.

Wisdom of the Ages

Eight teachers underwrite this work.

Each names a load-bearing principle. Together they form the intellectual scaffolding of the Pattern Language.

Christopher Alexander
A Pattern Language
Patterns of user action cannot be separated from the lane, surface, and stage where they occur. The central metaphor.
Edward Tufte
Small multiples
Same shape, different content, side-by-side. The eye learns the shape and then sees variation. The smallest effective difference.
Donald Norman
The Design of Everyday Things
Every element announces its function to every body, in every culture, in every modality.
Bret Victor
Direct manipulation
Stop drawing dead fish. Build tiny live instruments at every floor of every workflow.
Frank Lloyd Wright
Compression and release
The platform breathes the user through compression and release at the architecture-of-experience scale.
Mister Rogers
Look for the helpers
The platform surfaces helpers. It never replaces them. This is brand-defining.
Toyota Production System
Jidoka and Poka-yoke
Automate with a human touch. Design errors out of the system. The "eight Floor 0 questions" rule is poka-yoke for spec drift.
Steve Jobs and Jony Ive
The embodied constraint
Design relentlessly to the single most primal user constraint. For us, that constraint is the arrival stance in state space.
