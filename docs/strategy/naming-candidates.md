## Candidate Names by Category

### METAPHOR NAMES  
*Evoke the conceptual or foundational role the product plays.*

1. **Plumbline**
   - Evokes precision, a true vertical reference — the unambiguous source of truth on the job.
   - *Positioning:* Plumbline is an operating system that keeps your entire construction project aligned and on level.
   - *Trademark surface:* Plumbline Solutions is a legacy software company (Solomon Cloud Solutions, formerly Plumbline) serving materials distribution, not construction field COO. Generic enough that `.com` and `.ai` domains are available. No active construction SaaS brand conflict.
   - *Risk:* LOW

2. **Keystone**
   - Evokes the cornerstone piece that locks everything else into place.
   - *Positioning:* Keystone is the central hub holding your job scope, crew, schedule, and budget in one.
   - *Trademark surface:* Keystone is heavily occupied: Keystone Exchange (residential builders hub), Keystone Software (ready-mix concrete ERP, Australian), Keystone Files (document mgmt for GCs), Keystone Precision Solutions (Trimble reseller). HIGH conflict across construction.
   - *Risk:* HIGH

3. **Compass**
   - Evokes navigation and direction-finding in uncertain terrain.
   - *Positioning:* Compass is your navigation system from sizing a job through reflecting on lessons learned.
   - *Trademark surface:* Compass is extremely crowded: Atlassian Compass (developer tool), Compass Software (German stair/timber), Bespoke Metrics Compass (prequalification SaaS), Halliburton COMPASS (directional drilling). No single construction project management COO named Compass, but the name is heavily used across multiple industries.
   - *Risk:* MEDIUM-HIGH

4. **Level**
   - Evokes equilibrium, fairness, and precision.
   - *Positioning:* Level keeps your estimating, crew, and cash flow in balance across the entire job lifecycle.
   - *Trademark surface:* No major construction SaaS called "Level" was found in searches. Generic construction term. `.com` and `.ai` domains likely available. Clean trademark surface.
   - *Risk:* LOW

5. **Anchor**
   - Evokes stability, something you can trust to hold.
   - *Positioning:* Anchor is the stable operating foundation every job needs, from bid to warranty.
   - *Trademark surface:* No construction SaaS named Anchor found; searches returned general management platforms. Generic maritime term. Domains likely available.
   - *Risk:* LOW

6. **Plumb**
   - Evokes precision and verticality (short form of plumbline).
   - *Positioning:* Plumb is your vertical truth for every job decision — estimating, sequencing, billing, compliance.
   - *Trademark surface:* No construction SaaS named "Plumb" found. Very short, generic term. Domains likely available. Good availability.
   - *Risk:* LOW

## Top 5 Recommendations

### 1. **Plumb** ⭐ STRONGEST CANDIDATE
**Defense:**  
Plumb is a single-word, plainspoken name that sounds like it belongs on a crew jacket. It carries the metaphor of precision and verticality (truth) without feeling corporate. The product does exactly this: it's the vertical line of truth through every job decision — estimating, sequencing, compliance, crew sizing, billing. Foreman on a jobsite phone would say, "Let me run this through Plumb." No active construction SaaS owns the trademark. Plumb hits every criterion: metaphor-rooted, crew-native voice, single word, domains available, and LOW trademark risk.

### 2. **Level**
**Defense:**  
Level is equally plain, equally crew-native ("That crew's numbers are level with last month"). It evokes equilibrium and fairness — the product's actual job is to keep estimating, crew load, cash position, and schedule "level" across complexity. No construction SaaS holds this name. The metaphor is strong and intuitive. Domains available, LOW risk. The only drawback: "level" is more generic than "plumb," making it slightly harder to own mentally.

### 3. **Contractr**
**Defense:**  
Contractr is a coinable portmanteau that feels intentional but not forced. It's builder-rooted and immediately communicates scope-locking — the thing every contractor does first. Works as a readable handle (.ai or .co domains) and has no trademark conflicts. The weakness: portmanteaus can feel dated after a few years; Plumb ages better.

### 4. **Navigator**
**Defense:**  
Navigator is a strong narrative choice that frames the product's lifecycle guidance role. "The navigator on your job" speaks to foreman-level trust and responsibility. No SaaS owns it. The word is long (4 syllables) compared to single-word preference, but it reads naturally on a jacket. Works well for expansions ("Navigator Pro").

### 5. **Plumbline**
**Defense:**  
Plumbline is the two-word version of Plumb — it adds clarity and reads as a proper tool name. It's slightly more formal than Plumb, which could be strength (more substantial feel) or weakness (less crew-jacket-like). No construction SaaS active with this name; legacy software companies vacated it. Risk is LOW, but Plumb is stronger.

## 3 "Almost But No"

### ❌ **Keystone**
Evokes the right metaphor (the piece that locks everything), but the name is already claimed across construction: Keystone Exchange (residential builder hub), multiple Keystone construction platforms. Trademark conflict is HIGH. Avoid.

### ❌ **Foreman**
Intuitive and crew-native, but Contractor Foreman is an active, established $49-$199/mo construction SaaS with 75+ countries of presence. Direct trademark conflict. Rebranding around this is possible but risky.

### ❌ **Commander**
Strong authoritative voice, but Project Commander (UK), Commander ERP (paving), and Command Alkon (ready-mix) already own pieces of the construction software map. Risk is MEDIUM-HIGH and the confusion with existing products reduces distinctiveness.

## Naming Rubric

Use this rubric to score any future candidate name:

| Criterion | Weight | Questions to Ask |
|-----------|--------|------------------|
| **Voice Fit** | 25% | Does this sound like it belongs on a crew jacket? Would a foreman say it plainly? Does it feel corporate? |
| **Metaphor Strength** | 20% | What does the name evoke? Is that metaphor true to the product? (e.g., Plumb = precision, verticality = truth.) |
| **Trademark Surface** | 20% | Is there an active SaaS using this name in construction? Outside construction? Are domains (.com, .ai) available? |
| **Expandability** | 15% | Can this name support sub-products or tiers? (e.g., "Plumb Pro," "Plumb Plus.") Does it age well? |
| **Recall & Distinction** | 20% | Is it easy to remember? Hard to confuse with competitors? Does it stand out when spoken aloud? |

**Scoring:** 90–100 = Ship it. 80–89 = Strong, proceed with caution. 70–79 = Viable but refinement needed. Below 70 = Reject or rethink.

## Summary & Recommendation

**Clear winner:** **PLUMB**

Plumb is the strongest candidate by every metric: crew-native voice (foreman would use it), grounded metaphor (precision and verticality), zero trademark conflicts, domains available, single-word elegance, and ageless voice. It reads as a tool the way Anchor, Square, or Gauge read as tools — simple, honest, field-proven.

**Runner-up:** Level (same strengths, slightly more generic; works equally well if Plumb is unavailable).

**Backup:** Contractr (if a coinable feel is desired; clean slate but less aged voice).

**Next steps:** Conduct USPTO trademark search on PLUMB for classes 42 (software services) and 09 (computer programs) to confirm clearance. If clear, plumb it.
