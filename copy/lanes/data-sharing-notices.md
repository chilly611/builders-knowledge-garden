# Per-Lane Data-Sharing Notices

*Multi-lane MLP copy · 2026-05-28 · Status: DRAFT — consent surface, pending legal review (see flags)*

## Design constraints
- Light backgrounds globally; each Lane's notice renders on a warm-white #FAFAF8 card
- Archivo body, Archivo Black for the Lane name
- Two-section layout: "What you can see" then "What stays private"
- The "Why" line in italic Archivo, slightly smaller
- A small red Killer App badge with the Lane name at the top of each card
- Each notice displays at first sign-in for that Lane on each project; user must scroll-through-and-tap-acknowledge before continuing

---

## 1. Owner Lane (95 words)

**What you can see:**
- Total project budget — spent, committed, remaining
- All stages, milestones, inspections
- Photos, videos, and updates from the team
- Pay applications and change orders awaiting your approval
- Cast of everyone on the project
- The team's answers to your questions

**What stays private:**
- Your builder's profit margin on the project
- Pricing terms between your builder and subcontractors
- Your builder's other projects
- Internal notes between the builder and trades

*Why: Your builder needs working room to negotiate with subs and run their business. Everything that affects YOUR home and YOUR dollars stays visible to you.*

> ⚠ LEGAL FLAG — Primary consent surface for paying Owners. CCPA-compliant in CA. Construction counsel review required before paid sale.

---

## 2. GC Lane (95 words)

**What you can see:**
- Everything on this project — budget, contracts, sub terms, supplier pricing, owner conversations, drawings, RFIs, photos, schedules, inspections
- Your full pipeline of projects, leads, and Command Center
- Internal notes you author about subs, suppliers, and the project

**What stays private:**
- Other GCs' projects — the platform never shares your data with competitors
- Owner personal data they haven't chosen to share with you
- Lender-only data on construction draws unless the Owner has shared lender access

*Why: You run the project. You see everything that runs it.*

> ⚠ LEGAL FLAG — "The platform never shares your data with competitors" is a binding commitment. Confirm platform terms enforce this and that any future data partnerships are explicitly carved out.

---

## 3. Sub (Specialty Contractor) Lane (99 words)

**What you can see:**
- Your scope of work and contract on this project
- Your schedule, milestones, upcoming inspections
- Drawings and specs that affect your trade
- Your pay-app status, submissions, history
- Your RFIs and answers
- Photos relevant to your scope

**What stays private:**
- Total project budget and the Owner's financial details
- Other subcontractors' pricing and contract terms
- The Owner's contact information unless shared by your builder
- GC's profit margin on this project

*Why: You see what you need to work and get paid. Your builder protects everyone's privacy.*

> ⚠ LEGAL FLAG — Confirm platform is not deemed a party to the underlying sub-GC contract. The platform mediates information flow, not the contract itself.

---

## 4. Worker Lane (87 words)

**What you can see:**
- Today's task and this week's schedule
- Safety briefings for the scope you're on
- Your timesheet and pay history
- Voice notes and photos you submit
- Crew you're working alongside

**What stays private:**
- Project budget and contract terms
- Other workers' pay or schedules
- Owner contact information
- Sub or supplier financial terms

*Why: You see what you need to do good work safely and get paid for it. The rest belongs to your employer and the project team.*

> ⚠ LEGAL FLAG — Timesheet records carry state-specific retention and access requirements (CA labor law in particular). Employment counsel review required if platform stores timesheet data.

---

## 5. Supplier Lane (91 words)

**What you can see:**
- Purchase orders issued to your company
- Invoices, payment status, and history
- Delivery windows for your orders
- Project schedule milestones that affect deliveries
- Contact information for the GC's procurement lead

**What stays private:**
- Total project budget
- Other suppliers' pricing and terms
- Owner contact information
- Subcontractor pay terms
- GC's profit margin

*Why: You see what's relevant to fulfilling your orders and getting paid. Pricing and competition stay confidential between you, the GC, and your contracts.*

---

## 6. Architect / Engineer (Service Provider) Lane (96 words)

**What you can see:**
- All drawings and specifications you've issued
- RFIs and clarifications in context with the drawings
- Change orders that affect design
- Design intent vs. as-built status
- Inspection results relevant to your scope
- Code questions and resolutions

**What stays private:**
- Detailed project budget and contract terms
- Subcontractor and supplier pricing
- GC's profit margin
- Owner personal contact information unless shared

*Why: Your work is the drawings and the design. You see everything that helps your drawings get built right.*

> ⚠ LEGAL FLAG — Architects/Engineers carry professional liability. Confirm data-sharing through the platform does not interfere with stamp-of-record duties or contractual privity with the Owner.

---

## 7. Inspector Lane (90 words)

**What you can see:**
- Inspections scheduled, in progress, completed
- The scope of work being inspected
- Code references and citations relevant to the inspection
- Pass/fail history and required corrections
- Sign-off status

**What stays private:**
- Project budget and financial details
- Contract terms with the GC, subs, suppliers
- Owner personal information beyond what's required for the permit
- Other inspections on other projects

*Why: You see what you need to inspect and certify. Project economics are not relevant to code compliance.*

> ⚠ LEGAL FLAG — If inspector is a public AHJ official, platform must comply with public-records and jurisdictional access rules. Jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction; CA AHJs first for Marin demo.

---

## 8. Lender Lane (99 words including legal flag)

**What you can see:**
- Construction draws requested, approved, disbursed
- Draw documentation, photos, inspection sign-offs
- Stage completion percentage
- Loan-to-value pulse based on current build status
- Schedule of values tied to draws

**What stays private:**
- Detailed individual subcontractor pricing
- GC's profit margin
- Internal team communications
- Owner data beyond what's relevant to the loan agreement

*Why: You see what you need to release funds with confidence. Day-to-day project operations stay with the build team.*

> ⚠ LEGAL FLAG — HIGHEST RISK LANE. Intersects with construction lending regulation (TRID, state lending rules, fair-lending laws). Requires lending counsel (separate from construction counsel) before any paid Lender Lane access.
