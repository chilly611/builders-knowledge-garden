## "Who we serve" (use this between Plan and Build, or after the close)

### Geography

- We're starting in California, but the platform's jurisdictional logic covers **California, Nevada, Arizona, Texas, and Florida** out of the gate.
- Texas and Florida are the two highest-volume residential markets in the country right now — that's where the next wave of partners comes from.
- *"Most construction software is generic about codes. We're the opposite — local building department, headroom rules, permit constraints. They live inside the platform, not in a PDF the contractor printed last year."*

### Trades

- Not just general contractors. The platform is built for every trade with its own line items and compliance needs:
  - **Roofing**
  - **HVAC**
  - **Plumbing & electrical**
  - **Low-voltage** — AV, media systems, alarm integration (the trade that nobody else's software handles cleanly)
  - **Solar installers**
  - **Finishing trades** — cabinetry, millwork, high-end surfaces
- *"Every trade gets its own line item library, its own contract templates, its own jurisdictional compliance set. Roofers don't see plumbing line items they don't need; low-voltage techs don't fight a UI built for general contractors."*

### Project scale — the strongest talking point

The platform scales from the smallest residential job to enterprise development:

| Scale | Example | Why it works |
|---|---|---|
| **Micro** | Kitchen remodel, residential DIY | Plain-speak toggle hides jargon. Designed for a homeowner. |
| **Mid** | 1,800–4,000 sqft custom homes (like Marin) | Parcel-site autofills, ballpark estimating, budget-to-permit flow |
| **Macro** | Real estate developers — 400 to 1,200-unit master-planned tracks | Multi-user punch lists, deep change-order structures, enterprise cost tracking |
| **Mega** | 42+ floor skyscrapers, core data centers | Manual fallback branches, advanced structural variables, massive contract structures |

- *"Same operating system, different complexity exposed. You can show a homeowner doing a kitchen remodel the same platform that's tracking a 1,000-unit development across three counties. The UI scales — the underlying data model is the same."*
- *"That's what makes this a platform and not just a contractor tool."*

## "What the platform does today" (use when the doctor asks "what does it actually do")

Four concrete capabilities John can name:

### 1. The top ribbon — your project at a glance

- Spend, income, headroom, and the 7-stage journey, always visible at the top of every screen.
- Completion shown as circular progress rings, not color-coded states — readable in any light, on any device.
- Click any stage ring → go straight to that workspace. No nav tree, no menus.

### 2. Size Up & estimating

- **Plain Speak vs. Pro toggle** — same workspace, two vocabularies. Pro for contractors, Plain Speak for homeowners and clients.
- Type a project description, pick a profile (Home / Shop / Mix), get a ballpark budget in seconds.
- Change any input — square footage, location, finish level — every downstream line item recalculates instantly.

### 3. Drag-and-drop job sequencing

- Reorder phases by dragging — like rearranging stops on a multi-stop Uber.
- Sequence updates cascade through the timeline automatically.
- The platform flags impossible orderings (e.g., scheduling exterior stucco before interior framing is done) — *prevents the most expensive construction mistakes before they hit the field.*

### 4. The unified system of record

- Permit flow tracking — local building department, jurisdictional zoning, code constraints all visible in context, not buried in a navigation tree.
- Anywhere the platform doesn't have automation yet, it surfaces a clean manual fallback — attach notes, custom contract lines, external documents to the project record.
- *"Every project is its own knowledge base by the time you finish. The next one starts smarter because the last one taught it something."*
